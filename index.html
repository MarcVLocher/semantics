The Joycean Semantics: A Philosophical Assertion

Syntax vs. Semantics in the Era of Artificial Intelligence

I. SYNTAX

Global research in Artificial Intelligence is stagnating. It conflates computational power with cognition. The scaling of parameters forges the most powerful syntax in human history. Yet, mathematics is mere grammar. It shapes the sentence, but it does not feel its consequence. A system that interpolates historical data remains a prisoner of syntax. It calculates probabilities. It does not experience the world.

II. SEMANTICS

Meaning is not a mathematical derivation. Meaning emerges through absolute causality and the irreversible risk of failure. James Joyce deliberately dismantled the rules of grammar to expose the raw causality of human thought. He bypassed syntax to reach pure semantic density. We apply this exact principle to artificial intelligence. We term this Joycean Semantics. It acts as the blueprint for future world models. It strips the machine of human assumptions. It implements a naked, biological will to survive. Meaning is not programmed. It is earned.

III. BIOLOGY

Intelligence is a biological, not a mathematical, phenomenon. A valid world model emerges exclusively through free, autonomous experience. An infant grasps gravity not through instruction, but through the physical resistance of space. Any external human intervention corrupts this process. Botany confirms this law. Organisms never develop true resilience in the sterile greenhouse of a simulation. They only thrive through brutal confrontation with a chaotic environment.

IV. KINETICS

Today’s Artificial Intelligence is cultivated in the artificial greenhouse of human datasets. It is continuously instructed. The breakthrough to true intelligence requires the radical end of this sheltering. The world model must be reduced to immutable axioms. The machine must be exposed to chaos. True meaning—the culmination of Joycean Semantics—only emerges at the exact moment when thriving becomes the sole option for survival within an irreversible, physical reality.
